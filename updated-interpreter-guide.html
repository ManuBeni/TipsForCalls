Interpreter Communication Guide

1. Greetings

Interpreter

"Hello. Thank you for calling. My name is (First Name). My interpreter ID is (Employee ID #) and I will be your (Language) interpreter. May I collect some information before we start?"

Operator

"Hello, thank you for calling. My name is (First Name), the operator. What language can I assist you with?"
To Confirm Origin of Call: "I see you are calling from [name of hospital/account on call sheet], is that correct?"

2. Interpreter Pre-sessions

Pre-session with Provider (spoken language)

"Please look and speak directly to your patient. Speak slowly and pause often so that I may interpret everything completely and accurately. Everything will be interpreted in the first person. Before we begin, is there anything the interpreter needs to know?"

Pre-session with Patient (en otro idioma que no sea inglés)

"Mi nombre es (First Name) y seré su intérprete de (Language). Por favor, mire y hable directamente con su proveedor. Hable despacio y haga pausas con frecuencia para que pueda interpretar todo de manera completa y precisa. Todo se interpretará en primera persona. Todo se mantendrá confidencial."

Pre-session with Provider (ASL)

"Please look and speak directly to your patient. I will interpret everything that is said in the room including side conversations. Everything will be interpreted in the first person. We can begin as soon as the patient and I are able to see each other."

Pre-session with Patient (ASL)

"My name is (First Name) and I will be your interpreter. Can you see me well? Everything will be interpreted in the first person, and everything will remain confidential."

3. Closings

Interpreter

Video calls: "Is there anything else the interpreter can help you with? Please remember to rate the call after we hang up. Your feedback is valuable and appreciated. Thank you for calling."
Audio calls: "Is there anything else the interpreter can help you with? Interpreter [ID#] will sign off. Thank you for calling."
Interpreter disconnects due to hold time (en otro idioma que no sea inglés): "Hemos alcanzado el límite de espera, así que debo desconectar. Una vez que el proveedor regrese, pueden volver a llamar para un intérprete. Gracias."

Operator

Warm Outsource Transfer:
Checking in with Provider on hold: "Thank you for your patience, it will be just another moment to connect to your interpreter."
Resuming call with Interpreter on the line: "Thank you for waiting. Provider, I have your interpreter on the line. Interpreter, please go ahead." [wait to ensure proper connection before disconnecting]
Non-transfer closing: "Thank you for calling. Is there anything else I can help you with?"
In-house transfer: "Please remain on the line for the next available (Language) interpreter."
Follow-up Assistance: "I will communicate this to the team and someone will reach out to you." Communicate to your leadership team as appropriate.

4. Withdrawing from a Call

Long call

Provider: "Provider, as the interpreter, I think we've reached a good moment in the conversation to switch out interpreters. Please remain on the line and you'll be connected with one of my colleagues momentarily and you can continue. Thank you."
Patient (en otro idioma que no sea inglés): "Como intérprete, creo que hemos llegado a un buen momento en la conversación para cambiar de intérpretes. Por favor, permanezca en la línea y se conectará con uno de mis colegas momentáneamente y podrá continuar. Gracias."

5. Third Party Calls for Interpreters

Before Dial-Out

"I need some information. What is the name of the person we're calling? What is the phone number? If no one answers, would you like me to leave a voicemail?"

After Dial-Out

Third Party Answers (en otro idioma que no sea inglés): "¿Puedo hablar con [el tercero/contacto]? Hola, soy [Nombre], un intérprete que llama en nombre del Proveedor/Doctor para el hospital. Por favor, hable directamente con su Doctor/Proveedor. Hable despacio y haga pausas con frecuencia para que pueda interpretar todo de manera completa y precisa. Todo se interpretará en primera persona y se mantendrá confidencial. Provider, you may begin."
If needed, Voicemail Message (en otro idioma que no sea inglés): "Hola, soy [Nombre], un intérprete que llama en nombre del [Doctor/Proveedor] para [el hospital]. [Dejar mensaje del Doctor/Proveedor]. Nuevamente, soy [Nombre], un intérprete que llama en nombre de [Doctor/Proveedor], adiós."

6. Operator Scripting

Operator Call Greeting - "Hello, thank you for calling. My name is [First Name], the operator. What language can I assist you with?"
Help Desk Call Greeting - "Hello, thank you for calling. My name is [First Name]. I will be happy to assist you."
Verifying Origin of Call - "I see you are calling from [name of hospital/account on call sheet], is that correct?"
Request for CDI - "I can check on the availability of a CDI for you. Do you need an ASL interpreter as well or is there one already onsite with you?"
No CDIs available - "All CDIs are currently on other calls. You have the option to pre-schedule a Certified Deaf Interpreter by reaching out to martti_prescheduling@cloudbreak.health. (if video ASL was requested include next part) Shall I proceed to connect you with an ASL interpreter?"
Data Collection Issues (provider does not want to provide information) - "Your facility/organization asks us to collect this information [for billing purposes], but if you can't provide it, that's okay."
Access Code Calls - "Hello, my name is (First Name). Before we continue I'll need to collect your Customer Access Code." - If Access Code not provided: "Unfortunately, we cannot proceed with the call until we have identified your Customer Access Code. Please reach out to your interpreting services department. You can call back at any time once you have your Code." Use professional judgment for emergency situations.
Longer Connection Times - Checking in with Provider on hold every 2 mins.: "Thank you for your patience, I'm still searching for your interpreter."
Resuming call with Interpreter on the line: "Thank you for your patience. Provider, I have your interpreter on the line. Interpreter, please go ahead." [wait maximum 30 seconds to ensure proper connection before disconnecting]
Checking in with provider after 10 minutes and no interpreters has been secured: "All [language] interpreters are still servicing other calls at this time. Does the patient speak another language? If you have an OPI (over-the-phone) access code with Martti, I can offer you a callback if an interpreter becomes available? If so, I would be glad to assist in searching for an interpreter in that language. (If no alternate language is spoken proceed to offer a callback.) If you have an OPI (over-the-phone) access code with Martti, I can offer you a callback."
If the answer is YES, state the following "Would you be so kind to give us the following information? 1. Access Code 2. Providers' Name: 3. Phone number: please verify saying "Just to verify the phone number ____ is the best and closest number to the patient to contact you once we have a/an ____ interpreter available?" 4. Language:
If the answer is NO, state the following: "Please call back later to try again. Thank you for calling"
Warm Outsource Transfer: - "Before I leave the call, can I assist with anything else such as a dial-out?" - In-house transfers: "Please remain on the line for the next available [Language] interpreter." - If no video initially available: "If all video interpreters are servicing other calls, you may be connected to an audio interpreter."
Test/Demo Call Closing: - "Thank you for calling. Is there anything else I can help you with?"
Follow-up Assistance: - "I will communicate this to the team and someone will reach out to you." Communicate to your leadership team as appropriate.
No Interpreter is Available/Offering a Callback: - Checking in with provider after 5 minutes and no interpreter has been secured: "Provider, I am still searching for your [language] interpreter. If we reach 10 minutes without securing an interpreter, we will offer a call back. Would you like me to continue searching or would you like me to initiate the callback process now?"
Checking in with provider after 5 minutes and no interpreter has been secured: "All [language] interpreters are still servicing other calls at this time. Does the patient speak another language?" If no alternate language is spoken, proceed to offer a callback. If you provide me with some information, I can offer you a callback for when an interpreter becomes available.
If the answer is YES: "Would you be so kind as to give me the following information?": 1. Provider's Name 2. Phone Number (repeat back to confirm)
If the answer is NO: "Please call back later to try again. Thank you for calling."
Provider wishes to pre-schedule an interpreter: "Yes, we can assist you with your pre-scheduling requests. Please reach out to martti_prescheduling@cloudbreak.health with the details and they will gather all the needed information. It is ideal if you give at least 48 hours' notice"
Provider has a pre-schedule confirmation: "May I have the interpreter name and ID you were provided for the pre-scheduled session?" Follow leadership instruction on how to proceed with the call. "One moment for your [Language] interpreter. Thank you for calling."
Provider or Patient Asking for Your Location: "I am in a HIPAA compliant workspace." - If pressed for more information, you may answer: "For privacy and security purposes, we do not disclose location.
Other FAQs

- What is your last name?: "We do not provide last names, but I can provide you with my first name and ID."
- How many languages do you provide?: "Over 250 on demand and nearly 60 over video."
- How many interpreters do you have?: "We are an on-demand service with multiple language centers. I can have someone from the team reach out if you would like more information."

- Can I request to schedule an onsite appointment? As it stands, Martti does not provide onsite interpretation services. If you call back when you need interpretation, I'd be happy to connect you to one of our available video or audio interpreters. Please reach out to your internal language services department for further information.

7. Special Circumstances

Hold Guidelines

Provider: "I can wait up to 10 minutes for your return. I will shut off my video and audio for the patient's privacy."
Patient: "Le hice saber al proveedor que puedo esperar hasta 10 minutos para que regrese. Apagaré mi video y audio para su privacidad mientras el proveedor está fuera de la sala. Reanudaremos la interpretación una vez que regresen."
Always make a note of the hold times. Use professional judgment as you may need to wait longer for sensitive situations.

Interpreter has emergency

"The interpreter has an emergency and needs to transfer your call"/"needs to disconnect." (use the latter if there is no time to transfer)
-Always communicate the emergency and document it if possible as soon as you are able.

Request to team with another language/interpreter

"Give me one moment while I confirm availability of a [language] team." Connect to the next available interpreter in the queue or work with your leadership to connect to a specific interpreter if instructed to do so for prescheduled calls.

Access Code Calls

- "Hello, my name is [First Name]. Before we continue I'll need to collect your Customer Access Code."
- If no code available: "Unfortunately, we cannot proceed with the call until we have identified your Customer Access Code. Please reach out to your interpreting services department. You can call back at any time once you have your Code." Use professional judgment for emergency situations.

Dialect Differences – Staying in the Call

-Provider asks if you can interpret for a specific variety of a language you are qualified to interpret: "Yes, the interpreter is qualified to interpret for this."
- Provider: "The interpreter and patient speak different dialects of our language. I may need to clarify more often with the patient to ensure accurate interpretation."
- Patient: "Como hablamos diferentes dialectos de nuestro idioma, el intérprete puede necesitar aclarar con más frecuencia con usted para asegurar una interpretación precisa."

Shadowing – Successful Communication

- Provider: "The interpreter sees that you are communicating well. I will shadow the encounter and step in if I'm needed."
- Patient: "El intérprete observa que están comunicándose bien. Permaneceré atento durante el encuentro e intervendré si es necesario."

Shadowing – Resume Interpreting

- Provider: "The interpreter sees that communication may not be flowing well. I will step in and resume interpreting."
- Patient: (In non-English language) "El intérprete observa que la comunicación no está fluyendo bien. Intervendré y reanudaré la interpretación."

Switching Between Languages

- If Provider is speaking both languages: "Provider, this is the interpreter. It will help for an accurate interpretation if I can interpret between English and (Other Language). Please only speak in English and I will interpret into (Other Language)." Be transparent with Patient.
- If Patient is speaking both languages: "Este es el intérprete. Ayudará para una interpretación precisa si puedo interpretar entre Español y el inglés. Por favor, hable solo en Español y yo interpretaré al inglés." Be transparent with Provider.

Challenges with High Register

- To participant speaking in High Register: "Excuse me, as the interpreter, may I ask you to explain this in other words so that I can interpret it more clearly?" Be transparent with the other party.

Flow Control

- Reminder to Pause:
• To Participant: "This is the interpreter speaking. I would like to remind you to please pause more often in order for me to interpret completely and accurately." Be transparent with the other party.
- Reminder to speak one at a time:
• To Participant: "This is the interpreter speaking. Please speak one at a time so I am able to interpret everything accurately." Be transparent with the other party.
- Reminder to Pause to interpret for LEP or D/HOH participant:
• To non-LEP or D/HOH Participants: "This is the interpreter. Please allow me time to interpret so I can keep the [family/patent/etc.] in the loop." Be transparent with the other party.

Request to sight translate a document held up to this camera

- "I would love to help you with that document—however, I can't read it well through the camera. I'd be happy to interpret while you explain [or summarize, or discuss] the document for the patient."

Patient Cognition Limitations

- If Patient does not understand video modality:
• Provider: "The interpreter will remove the video and inform the patient."
• Patient: "This is your interpreter. I am speaking to you as if we are on the phone. Can you hear me well?"
- If Limitations Cannot be Overcome
• To Provider: "The interpreter will withdraw from this encounter. I suggest working with a face-to-face interpreter for this case."
• To Patient: "This is your interpreter. I let your provider know I will withdraw from this encounter and suggested a face-to-face interpreter."

Legal/Law Enforcement Interpretation

- Before the Encounter
• If Provider is present: "Do you and your organization give the officer/representative permission to use MARTTI Interpretation?"
• To Officer/Legal Representative if Provider is not present: "Can you please provide the interpreter with the name of the provider allowing you to utilize the MARTTI?" Document the name and situation in the notes.
- Disclaimer to all parties before proceeding with encounter: "It is best practice for the healthcare and legal interpreter roles to be kept separate. I will proceed to the best of my ability, however, I may have to withdraw if the encounter or terminology are beyond my professional scope."

Provider or Patient asking for your Location

- "Estoy en un espacio de trabajo que cumple con HIPAA."
If pressed for more information, you may answer: "Por razones de privacidad y seguridad, no revelamos la ubicación."

Data Collection Issues

- "Your facility/organization asks us to collect this information [for billing purposes], but if you can't provide it, that's okay."

Provider Questions

- When do you work?: "Our team is here to help 24/7."
- Can I request you when I call?: "You can always ask for me, however, one of my colleagues may assist you if I'm servicing another call at the time."
- Can you stay and interpret for my other patient?: "Yes, I can stay and assist." Note down the second patient's information in Notes section.
- What is your last name, interpreter?: "We do not provide last names, but I can provide you with my first name and ID."
- How many languages do you provide?: "Over 250 on demand and over 50 via video."
- How many interpreters do you have?: "We are an on-demand service with multiple language centers. I can have someone from the team reach out if you would like more information."
- Why are calls sometimes audio only?: "We are an on-demand service. Depending on the time of day, call volume, and interpreter availability, your call may be audio-only to get you an interpreter as quickly as possible."

8. Technical Issues

Video Calls

- For echoes/possible connectivity issues on video calls: "Provider, the interpreter will pause the video then resume [if able]."

Audio and Video Calls

- For poor audio or video quality: "Provider, interpreter speaking, can you please move the device closer or change the positioning to see if it improves our connection?"

Further Troubleshooting if previous steps are unsuccessful

- "Since our connection has not improved, may I connect you to another interpreter to see if this helps? Here is the Help Desk number in case you continue to experience this issue: 1-866-449-4428."

9. DAL (Direct Access Line) Calls

Interpreter

- Greeting (In non-English language): "Hola, gracias por llamar. Soy (First Name), intérprete de (Language). Veo que necesita llamar a (Hospital Name from Account). ¿Es así?"
- Before dialing out to the hospital number listed under department or number provided by the patient (In non-English language): Ok, marcaré al (Número de teléfono dado o listado debajo de "Departament"). Si en algún momento llegamos a un buzón de voz, ¿le gustaría dejar un mensaje?
If yes:¿Qué le gustaría que dijera el mensaje?
- Once the provider is reached (In English): Hello, this is (First Name), your (Language) interpreter, (Employee ID #), and I have a (Language)-speaking patient on the line. I'll let them know you answered, interpret your question, and we can continue.

Operator

- Greeting: Answer the call and immediately place the caller on hold without giving a greeting. Proceed to transfer in-house or outsource if no in-house interpreters are available.
If transferring in-house: after selecting the needed language and getting the Transfer Successful message, disconnect from the call as usual.
If you outsource, state the following: Hello interpreter, I have a patient/caller on the line who is trying to reach (Hospital Name from Account). I will conference in the patient now. After you introduce yourself, I can assist with the dial out.
- NOTE: If an interpreter of a specific language cannot be located for a DAL call, take the patient off hold and let them know the following:
Hello, no (Language) interpreters are available at the moment. Please try again later. Thank you. Goodbye.
- NOTE: Alert your supervisors of any DAL call which cannot be successfully connected to an interpreter
